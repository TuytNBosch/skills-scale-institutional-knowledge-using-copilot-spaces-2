# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads design and manage testing strategies to ensure deliverables meet quality criteria. They coordinate with product owners and developers for defect resolution and maintain quality standards throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design comprehensive testing strategies for projects
- Define quality criteria and acceptance thresholds
- Coordinate defect triage and resolution with developers
- Manage test environments and test data
- Review and approve test coverage metrics
- Facilitate QA handoffs and sign-offs for releases

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables that meet acceptance criteria
- Minimize production defects through proactive testing
- Establish efficient testing processes and automation

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with developers on defects and test results
- Weekly quality reviews with Project Manager and Product Owner
- Test status reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Pre-release quality sign-off documentation

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test strategies, defect resolution, and test automation
- **Product Owner**: Validate acceptance criteria and quality standards
- **Change Manager**: Review impact of changes on quality and testing scope
- **Automation Specialist**: Partner on test automation implementation
- **Project Manager**: Report on quality status, risks, and release readiness

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers own the process for managing project changes, ensuring all changes are properly documented, communicated to stakeholders, and assessed for impact. They facilitate impact analysis and maintain change logs.

### Responsibilities
- Establish and maintain change management processes
- Document all proposed and approved changes
- Facilitate change impact analysis with relevant stakeholders
- Communicate changes to all affected parties
- Maintain change logs and audit trails
- Coordinate change approval workflows

### Goals
- Ensure controlled and transparent change processes
- Minimize disruption from changes through proper planning
- Maintain comprehensive change documentation and history

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews with Project Manager and stakeholders
- Impact analysis sessions with technical and business teams
- Change notifications and status updates to all stakeholders
- Weekly change log reviews in project syncs

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Align on change priorities, timelines, and stakeholder communication
- **Product Owner**: Validate business impact and prioritization of changes
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Coordinate testing scope adjustments for changes
- **Stakeholder Liaison**: Ensure comprehensive stakeholder communication
- **Developers**: Assess technical feasibility and implementation impact

---

## Stakeholder Liaison

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Liaisons serve as the primary contact for stakeholder input and feedback. They consolidate requirements and concerns, communicate project progress and risks to relevant groups, and ensure stakeholder alignment.

### Responsibilities
- Serve as primary point of contact for stakeholder engagement
- Consolidate stakeholder requirements and feedback
- Communicate project status, progress, and risks
- Schedule and facilitate stakeholder meetings
- Document stakeholder decisions and commitments
- Identify and escalate stakeholder concerns

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder relationships and engagement
- Ensure timely and accurate stakeholder communication
- Achieve stakeholder alignment on project decisions and direction

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder status updates and briefings
- Monthly stakeholder review meetings
- Ad-hoc communications for urgent issues or decisions
- Stakeholder feedback summaries and action items

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate overall stakeholder strategy and messaging
- **Product Owner**: Gather and communicate product requirements and feedback
- **Change Manager**: Facilitate stakeholder communication on changes
- **Developers**: Channel technical questions and clarifications
- **Stakeholders**: Direct interface for all project-related communication

---

## Automation Specialist

### Role Summary
Automation Specialists identify opportunities for automating repeatable processes, implement CI/CD pipelines, and maintain automation scripts and tools to ensure process efficiency and consistency.

### Responsibilities
- Identify automation opportunities in development and deployment processes
- Design and implement CI/CD pipelines
- Create and maintain automation scripts and tools
- Configure automated testing and quality gates
- Monitor and optimize automation performance
- Train team members on automation tools and practices

### Goals
- Maximize process efficiency through automation
- Reduce manual errors and inconsistencies
- Enable faster, more reliable delivery cycles

### Typical Communication
- Technical discussions with developers on automation needs
- Pipeline status reports and metrics
- Documentation of automation tools and processes
- Training sessions on CI/CD best practices

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on build automation, testing frameworks, and deployment scripts
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Implement automated testing strategies and quality gates
- **Project Manager**: Report on automation progress and efficiency gains
- **Change Manager**: Automate change tracking and deployment processes
- **Product Owner**: Demonstrate automation benefits and delivery improvements
